community circles

monthly circles for our community

Each month, by their side hosts community circles for Indo-Caribbean and South Asian young people, ages 19–25, across the gender spectrum. These circles are a casual, grounded space to talk about our culture, our relationships, and the ways violence shows up in our lives — from the small comments that sting to the bigger patterns we’re trying to unlearn.

Circles are not workshops or lectures. They’re conversations. We sit together, share stories if we want to, listen to each other, and make room for both heaviness and joy. It’s a non-judgmental, brave space where you don’t have to have the “right” words to belong.

how we hold conversation

We lean into arts-based ways of speaking and listening — using writing, drawing, music, and storytelling as tools to express what doesn’t always fit neatly into sentences. You never have to share your work out loud if you don’t want to.

Our starting point is always the gifts our community already holds: our humour, our creativity, our language, our aunties’ stories, our friendships, our faith, our resilience. Circles aren’t about “fixing” people — they’re about building on what’s already here and imagining what safer, more caring relationships could look like together.

who it’s for

monthly community circles are for:

  • Indo-Caribbean and South Asian youth (19–25)
  • young women, men, and gender-diverse people
  • anyone curious about culture, care, and relationships

what to expect

  • casual, conversation-based sessions
  • arts-based prompts (writing, drawing, music, etc.)
  • a non-judgmental, brave space — share only what feels right
  • free to attend, hosted in community spaces in Peel Region
