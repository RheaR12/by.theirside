Unabated

aiding youth in building healthier relationships for a future free of relationship violence

about the workshop

Unabated is a community-rooted, discussion-based workshop designed to help young people deepen their understanding of relationships, identity, culture, and safety. Adapted for both high-school and post-secondary youth, it meets participants where they are — centering lived experience, curiosity, and collective wisdom.

what this workshop looks like

  • no lectures — just conversation. Youth shape the direction of the workshop.
  • arts-based learning. Drawing, writing, building, and storytelling support self-expression.
  • community-rooted knowledge. Everyone — youth, facilitators, teachers — is an expert.
  • invitational participation. Youth can step in and out of activities as needed.
  • anonymous options. Prompts that allow reflection without speaking out loud.

what youth learn

  • critical media literacy and the messages shaping their understanding of relationships
  • how to recognize subtle and non-physical forms of harm
  • emotional regulation, self-care, and self-trust
  • how to build connection, boundaries, and safer relationships

structure & details

  • length: 60–90 minutes
  • format: interactive, discussion-based
  • activities: real scenarios, role-play, art-based prompts
  • adapted for: high-school and post-secondary youth
  • cost: free for schools & community groups in 2025
